Build a web-based top-down pixel JRPG language learning game called “Hongdae Korean Quest”.

The game is a cozy Korean-learning RPG set in Hongdae, Seoul. The player explores a pixel-art version of Hongdae, walks around the neighborhood, talks to NPCs, interacts with shops and street objects, and learns Korean through real-life conversations.

The concept is like “Duolingo turned into a cozy pixel JRPG world.” Instead of learning Korean through boring flashcards or quiz screens, the player learns by actually using Korean inside a small explorable town. Every shop, NPC, sign, object, and quest should become part of the learning experience.

The player is a foreign visitor or exchange student who just arrived in Hongdae. They do not know much Korean yet. Their goal is to slowly become more confident by talking to locals, ordering food, asking for directions, making friends, shopping, reading signs, and completing conversation-based quests.

The game should feel warm, cozy, playful, and nostalgic, with the emotional feeling of a cute pixel RPG town. The visual inspiration should be a cozy 16-bit / 32-bit top-down pixel-art RPG with the same kind of comforting, charming, detailed pixel atmosphere as Stardew Valley, but all assets, characters, buildings, UI, and tiles must be original and not copied from any existing game.

Use a top-down orthographic view only. Do not make it side-scrolling. Do not make it 3D. Do not use isometric perspective. The player should view the world from above, like a classic cozy pixel RPG. The camera follows the player smoothly as they walk through the Hongdae map.

The world should be tile-based and explorable. The player can walk in four directions: up, down, left, and right. The character should have a cute chibi pixel-art design, about 2 tiles tall, with simple walking animations for each direction. The player should feel small inside a lively neighborhood, but the map should stay readable and not feel too crowded.

The main map is a compact pixel-art version of Hongdae, Seoul. It should include recognizable Hongdae-inspired elements:
- A Hongdae subway exit at the bottom of the map
- A busy pedestrian street in the center
- Small indie cafes

- Crosswalks
- Side alleys
- Outdoor cafe tables
- Cute shop signs in Korean
- Small decorative plants, bikes, scooters, and trash bins

The environment should feel like Hongdae at golden hour or early evening. It should have cozy warm lighting, colorful signs, soft street ambience, and a friendly youthful vibe. The mood should feel like a fun weekend walk in Seoul, not a dark cyberpunk city.

The map should feel alive. Add NPCs walking around, standing near shops, chatting, busking, waiting in line, ordering food, taking photos, or studying at cafes. NPCs should not feel random. Each NPC should have a learning purpose.

The main gameplay loop:
1. The player explores Hongdae.
2. The player sees an NPC or object with an interaction prompt.
3. The player presses E to interact.
4. A dialogue box opens.
5. The NPC gives a real-life Korean conversation situation.
6. The player chooses or types the correct Korean phrase.
7. The game gives friendly feedback.
8. The player earns XP, coins, friendship points, or unlocks a phrase.
9. The phrase is saved into the player’s phrasebook.
10. The player continues exploring and unlocks more conversations.

The learning should be practical and situation-based. The game should teach Korean that a real beginner would use in Seoul:
- Ordering coffee
- Buying street food
- Asking prices
- Asking directions
- Introducing yourself
- Making casual friends
- Buying convenience store items
- Asking for a bag
- Saying thank you politely
- Complimenting music
- Asking if you can take a photo
- Booking a noraebang room
- Reading basic Korean signs
- Understanding common casual expressions

Do not make the game feel like a normal quiz app. The learning should be embedded naturally inside the RPG world. The player should feel like they are learning Korean because they need it to progress through the world.

The tone should be friendly, cozy, casual, and motivating. Avoid overly academic explanations. Make the learning feel like a friend is helping you survive and enjoy Hongdae.
