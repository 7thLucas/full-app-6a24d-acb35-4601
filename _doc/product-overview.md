# Hongdae Korean Quest — Product Overview

## What it is

Hongdae Korean Quest is a web-based, top-down pixel JRPG for learning Korean. It is a
cozy language-learning RPG set in a compact, explorable pixel-art version of Hongdae,
Seoul. The player walks around the neighborhood, talks to NPCs, interacts with shops and
street objects, and learns Korean through real-life conversations embedded in the world.

The concept in one line: **"Duolingo turned into a cozy pixel JRPG world."** Instead of
flashcards or quiz screens, the player learns by actually using Korean inside a small
living town. Every shop, NPC, sign, object, and quest is part of the learning experience.

## Who it is for

The player is a **foreign visitor or exchange student** who has just arrived in Hongdae
and does not know much Korean yet. The goal is to slowly become more confident by talking
to locals, ordering food, asking for directions, making friends, shopping, reading signs,
and completing conversation-based quests. The target learner is a **real beginner**.

## Core fantasy

The player should feel like they are walking around Hongdae and slowly becoming more
confident speaking Korean. Every small conversation should feel like progress. The game
makes Korean learning feel like exploration, discovery, friendship, and small wins —
learning because you *need* the language to progress through the world.

## Tone & emotional target

Warm, cozy, playful, casual, motivating, and nostalgic — the emotional feeling of a cute
pixel RPG town. The mood is a fun weekend walk in Seoul at golden hour / early evening, not
a dark cyberpunk city. Learning should feel like a friend is helping you survive and enjoy
Hongdae. Avoid overly academic explanations.

## The gameplay loop

1. The player explores Hongdae.
2. They see an NPC or object with an interaction prompt.
3. They press **E** to interact.
4. A dialogue box opens.
5. The NPC presents a real-life Korean conversation situation.
6. The player chooses (or types) the correct Korean phrase.
7. The game gives friendly feedback.
8. The player earns XP, coins, friendship points, or unlocks a phrase.
9. The phrase is saved into the player's **phrasebook**.
10. The player keeps exploring and unlocks more conversations.

## What it teaches

Practical, situation-based Korean a real beginner would use in Seoul:

- Ordering coffee
- Buying street food
- Asking prices
- Asking directions
- Introducing yourself
- Making casual friends
- Buying convenience store items
- Asking for a bag
- Saying thank you politely
- Complimenting music
- Asking if you can take a photo
- Booking a noraebang room
- Reading basic Korean signs
- Understanding common casual expressions

## The world: a compact pixel Hongdae

A top-down orthographic, tile-based, explorable map. Compact but dense, alive, and full of
meaningful places. Arranged like a small walkable neighborhood:

- **Bottom** — Hongdae subway exit, stairs, subway sign with Korean text, street map
  board, crosswalk entrance, vending machine, bus stop sign, street lamps, posters.
- **Center** — main pedestrian street, wide enough to roam; NPCs walking, standing,
  chatting, waiting; street musicians and students; food carts; trees and benches; parked
  bikes and scooters; crosswalk markings; warm-lit shopfronts.
- **Left** — indie cafe and study cafe, outdoor tables, chalkboard menu, plants, students
  sitting outside, a barista, an interactable menu board, warm cafe windows.
- **Right** — convenience store, photo booth, K-fashion boutique, clothing displays,
  bright store lights, visible product shelves, a cashier, small neon signs.
- **Top** — busking zone with a small performance stage, guitar player / singer, a small
  crowd, speakers and mic stand, guitar case, colorful wall, posters and stickers.
- **Side alley** — narrow but walkable, neon Korean signs, coin noraebang entrance,
  graffiti wall, poster wall, vending machine, urban props, a slightly more colorful
  nightlife feel, noraebang staff near the entrance.

## Characters (NPCs)

Every NPC has a learning purpose — none are random. At least six:

1. **Cafe Barista** (indie cafe) — ordering coffee.
2. **Street Food Vendor** (street food stall) — buying food, asking price.
3. **Korean Student** (pedestrian street / study cafe) — introductions, casual chat.
4. **Convenience Store Cashier** (convenience store) — payment, shopping phrases.
5. **Busker Musician** (busking zone) — compliments, casual expressions.
6. **Noraebang Staff** (coin noraebang alley) — booking, time-related phrases.

Optional extras: fashion store clerk, tourist friend, subway helper, photo booth friend,
study cafe student, random passerby.

## Interactive objects

Each object teaches a phrase, unlocks vocabulary, gives a small cultural note, or triggers
a quest — never random. At least eight:

1. **Cafe menu board** — coffee vocabulary & ordering (e.g. 아메리카노, 라떼, 아이스, 따뜻한, 하나 주세요).
2. **Street food cart** — food vocabulary (떡볶이, 오뎅, 김밥, 얼마예요?, 하나 주세요).
3. **Subway map** — directions & stations (어디예요?, 왼쪽, 오른쪽, 직진, 홍대입구역).
4. **Convenience store fridge** — snack & drink vocabulary.
5. **Photo booth machine** — casual friend & photo phrases (사진 찍자, 같이 찍을래?, 귀엽다, 다시 찍자).
6. **Noraebang sign** — singing-room & reservation phrases (노래방, 한 시간, 두 명, 예약할 수 있어요?).
7. **Busking stage** — compliments & reaction words.
8. **Korean poster wall** — reading simple Korean words.
9. **Vending machine** — buying phrases.
10. **Graffiti wall** — slang & casual expressions.

## Systems

- **Movement** — WASD and arrow keys; walk up/down/left/right with per-direction walking
  animations and an idle animation. Grid-aligned / tile-based feel; smooth but never
  sliding unnaturally and never rotating like a 3D object.
- **Player character** — cute chibi pixel proportions, about 2 tiles tall, always clear
  and readable.
- **Collision** — cannot walk through buildings, walls, tables, stalls, NPCs, benches,
  lamps, vending machines, scooters, bikes, trees, bins, stage equipment, shelves, railings,
  the photo booth machine, signs, or decorative props. Can walk on streets, sidewalks,
  crosswalks, plazas, alleys, entry zones, and crowd areas with space. Paths stay clear so
  the player never gets stuck.
- **Camera** — follows the player smoothly, always top-down; no zoom, rotation, or shake;
  keeps the player near center with slight lookahead offset; bounded to the map edges.
- **Interaction** — a pixel-style "Press E to interact" prompt appears only when close
  enough; E opens NPC dialogue, an object learning card, a mini phrase lesson, a quest
  trigger, or an environmental description.
- **Progression** — XP, coins, friendship points, phrase unlocks, and a persistent
  phrasebook.

## Art direction

Cozy 16-bit / 32-bit top-down pixel-art RPG. Original pixel tiles, original character
sprites, cute chibi proportions, warm colors with soft pastel accents over warm evening
tones, detailed shop exteriors, pixel Korean street signs, cute UI panels, smooth simple
walking animation. Clear and readable — lively like Hongdae but not visually messy. Use
visual differences between walkable areas, non-walkable objects, interactive objects, and
decorative background elements. Environmental detail: neon lights, music posters, indie
band flyers, cafe chalkboards, street fashion, busking crowd, food steam, soft evening
glow, light reflections, gentle sign flickers, small idle NPC animations.

The visual inspiration is the comforting, charming, detailed cozy feel of Stardew Valley —
**feeling only**. All assets, characters, buildings, UI, tiles, maps, and sprites must be
**original** and not copied from any existing game.

## UI direction

A cozy RPG HUD, not a corporate learning platform: pixel-style panels, small icons, soft
rounded boxes, readable text. On screen: Level, XP bar, Streak, Coins, Current quest,
Interaction prompt, Phrasebook button, and a Dialogue box when talking to NPCs.

## Strategic principles & scope guardrails

- View is **top-down orthographic only** — never side-scrolling, 3D, isometric,
  cinematic, or first-person.
- Learning is **embedded naturally** in the world — never a quiz app, flashcard screen, or
  generic mobile-quiz / SaaS-dashboard layout.
- This is **not a farming game**. No farming, crops, barns, farm animals, watering cans, or
  farm tools — the Stardew reference is the cozy top-down RPG *feeling* only.
- No combat, monsters, weapons, dungeon mechanics, dark fantasy, or cyberpunk overload.
- No realistic 3D buildings or rendering.
- All art is original and clearly set in Hongdae, Seoul.
